## Overview

This document provides the complete workflow for triage doctor intakes at Forgotten Formula PMA. The triage doctor is the critical first point of clinical contact, responsible for comprehensive assessment and appropriate routing of new members.

---

## Part 1: Pre-Intake Preparation

### 1.1 Before the Appointment

**Review Available Documentation:**
- Membership application and health questionnaire
- Previous medical records (if provided)
- Lab work and imaging (if provided)
- Medication list
- Supplement list
- Chief complaints noted during scheduling

**System Preparation:**
- Open member profile in ALLIO
- Review ATHENA notes from support interactions
- Check for flagged concerns from intake team
- Prepare documentation templates

## Part 2: The Intake Consultation

### 2.1 Opening (5 minutes)

**Welcome and Set Expectations:**
"Welcome to Forgotten Formula. I'm Dr. [Name], and I'll be conducting your comprehensive intake today. This will take about 60-90 minutes. We're going to go through everything thoroughly so we can understand your complete health picture and create the best path forward for you."

**Establish Rapport:**
- Acknowledge they've taken a significant step
- Validate their health journey so far
- Create a safe space for honest disclosure

### 2.2 Chief Complaint Deep Dive (15 minutes)

**Primary Concern Assessment:**
1. What is the main issue bringing you to us today?
2. When did this first begin? (Timeline construction)
3. What makes it better or worse?
4. How does this affect your daily life?
5. On a scale of 1-10, how much does this impact your quality of life?
6. What have you already tried? (Treatments, supplements, lifestyle)
7. What do you believe is causing this?

**Secondary Concerns:**
- "What else is bothering you that we should address?"
- "Are there other symptoms you've learned to live with?"
- Capture ALL concerns, even those they've normalized

### 2.3 Complete Systems Review (20 minutes)

**Use the FFPMA 12-System Protocol:**

1. **Neurological**
   - Brain fog, memory, concentration
   - Sleep quality and patterns
   - Mood: anxiety, depression, irritability
   - Neuropathy, numbness, tingling

2. **Cardiovascular**
   - Blood pressure history
   - Palpitations, chest pain
   - Circulation issues
   - Family history of heart disease

3. **Respiratory**
   - Shortness of breath
   - Chronic cough
   - Asthma/allergies
   - Sleep apnea

4. **Gastrointestinal**
   - Digestion quality
   - Bowel movements (frequency, consistency)
   - Bloating, gas, pain
   - Food sensitivities
   - Acid reflux

5. **Genitourinary**
   - Urinary frequency/urgency
   - Infections
   - Sexual function
   - Menstrual history (if applicable)

6. **Musculoskeletal**
   - Joint pain/stiffness
   - Muscle pain
   - Mobility limitations
   - Previous injuries

7. **Dermatological**
   - Skin conditions
   - Rashes, eczema, psoriasis
   - Hair loss
   - Nail changes

8. **Endocrine**
   - Thyroid symptoms
   - Blood sugar regulation
   - Hormone symptoms
   - Energy patterns

9. **Immune**
   - Frequency of illness
   - Autoimmune conditions
   - Allergies
   - Chronic infections

10. **Dental/Oral**
    - Dental work history
    - Root canals, amalgams
    - Gum disease
    - Jaw issues (TMJ)

11. **Sensory**
    - Vision changes
    - Hearing issues
    - Taste/smell changes

12. **Psychological**
    - Stress levels
    - Trauma history
    - Coping mechanisms
    - Support systems

### 2.4 Lifestyle Assessment (10 minutes)

**Nutrition:**
- Typical daily diet (morning to night)
- Water intake
- Alcohol consumption
- Caffeine intake
- Recent diet changes
- Known deficiencies

**Movement/Exercise:**
- Current activity level
- Type of exercise
- Limitations
- Goals

**Sleep:**
- Hours per night
- Sleep quality
- Waking patterns
- Sleep aids used

**Stress & Environment:**
- Work stress level
- Relationship stress
- Environmental exposures
- Living situation
- Chemical exposures (home, work)

### 2.5 Medical History (10 minutes)

**Previous Diagnoses:**
- Chronic conditions
- Acute conditions in past 5 years
- Surgeries (list all)
- Hospitalizations
- Imaging/procedures

**Family History:**
- Parents' health conditions
- Siblings' health conditions
- Grandparents (if known)
- Hereditary patterns

**Medication Review:**
- Current prescriptions (dose, frequency, duration)
- OTC medications
- Supplements (brand, dose)
- Recent changes
- Adherence issues

**Allergy Review:**
- Drug allergies (reaction type)
- Food allergies
- Environmental allergies
- Supplement sensitivities

## Part 3: Clinical Assessment

### 3.1 Vitals (5 minutes)
- Blood pressure (both arms if indicated)
- Heart rate
- Temperature
- Respiratory rate
- Pulse oximetry
- Weight
- Height
- BMI calculation

### 3.2 Physical Observations
- General appearance and demeanor
- Skin quality and color
- Visible inflammation markers
- Posture and movement quality
- Eye and nail assessment (if trained)

### 3.3 Preliminary Testing Recommendations

**Standard Panel Recommendations:**
Based on intake findings, recommend appropriate testing:

| Finding/Concern | Recommended Testing |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Fatigue, low energy | CBC, CMP, thyroid panel, iron studies |
| Digestive issues | Comprehensive stool analysis, food sensitivity |
| Hormonal symptoms | Complete hormone panel |
| Cognitive issues | Organic acids, heavy metals |
| Inflammation | hs-CRP, ESR, cytokine panel |
| Cardiovascular concerns | Lipid panel, homocysteine, Lp(a) |

**Live Blood Analysis:**
- Recommend for all new members as baseline
- Provides immediate visual feedback
- Helps guide nutritional recommendations

## Part 4: Documentation

### 4.1 ALLIO System Entry

**Required Fields:**
- Chief complaint summary
- Systems review findings
- Current medications
- Allergies (FLAGGED prominently)
- Vital signs
- Assessment notes
- Initial recommendations

**HIPPOCRATES Agent Notification:**
After intake, trigger HIPPOCRATES to:
- Review intake documentation
- Generate preliminary protocol suggestions
- Flag any concerning patterns
- Prepare educational materials for member

### 4.2 Member Summary

Provide member with:
- Written summary of intake findings
- Testing recommendations and rationale
- Initial protocol suggestions
- Next steps and timeline
- Contact information for questions

## Part 5: Routing and Follow-Up

### 5.1 Complexity Classification

**Level 1 - Straightforward:**
- Single primary concern
- No major complicating factors
- Clear protocol path
- Route to: Standard practitioner follow-up

**Level 2 - Moderate:**
- Multiple concerns
- Some complexity
- May need multiple modalities
- Route to: Senior practitioner or specialist

**Level 3 - Complex:**
- Multiple chronic conditions
- Polypharmacy
- Autoimmune components
- Previous treatment failures
- Route to: Clinical director or Trustee review

**Level 4 - Critical:**
- Red flags present
- Potential medical emergency
- Beyond PMA scope
- Route to: Immediate conventional care referral

### 5.2 Red Flags Requiring Immediate Action

**STOP and refer to emergency care if:**
- Chest pain with radiation or shortness of breath
- Sudden severe headache ("worst of life")
- Neurological deficits (weakness, slurred speech)
- Severe abdominal pain with rigidity
- Active suicidal ideation with plan
- Signs of acute infection with fever >103°F

### 5.3 Follow-Up Scheduling

**Standard Timeline:**
- Testing: Schedule within 1 week
- Results review: 2-3 weeks from testing
- Protocol initiation: After results review
- Progress check: 4-6 weeks into protocol
- LBA follow-up: 4-8 weeks for comparison
