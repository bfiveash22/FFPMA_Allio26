# Live Blood Analysis System: User Guide

Welcome to the **Live Blood Analysis System**, integrated within the FFPMA Doctor Portal. This system allows you to connect USB microscopes directly to your browser, leverage real-time AI to identify anomalies in blood cells, and automatically generate comprehensive patient protocols without leaving your portal.

### 1. Initial Setup & Device Connection
When you first open the Live USB Microscope tool, the system will request permission to access your camera. 
* **Allow Camera Access**: Click "Allow" when prompted by your browser. This enables the system to detect connected USB microscopes.
* **Select Your Microscope**: Use the dropdown menu located at the top-right of the analyzer panel to select your specific USB microscope. If you have multiple cameras (like a built-in webcam and a microscope), ensure the external microscope is selected.

### 2. Operating the Live Feed
* **Connect Feed**: Once your device is selected, click the purple **"Connect Feed"** button. The live video from your microscope will appear in the main monitor.
* **Live Indicator**: A red pulsating "LIVE" indicator will appear in the top-left corner of the video feed, confirming the stream is active.
* **Disconnect**: Click the red **"Disconnect"** button when you are finished inspecting a slide to power off the camera and stop the stream.

### 3. Using the AI Analysis Tools
The analyzer offers two modes of AI detection powered by advanced computer vision:

* **Autonomous AI Analysis (Continuous Scanning)**: 
  * Click **"Start Autonomous AI"** to enable continuous scanning. 
  * In this mode, the system captures and analyzes a frame every 5 seconds.
  * *Best used for*: Real-time scanning while you are actively moving the slide around under the microscope to find anomalies automatically.
* **Snapshot Analysis (Manual Scanning)**:
  * Click **"Snapshot Analysis"** to take a single, instantaneous capture of the current frame and analyze it. 
  * *Best used for*: When you have found a very clear field of view and want to run a precise scan on that static image without continuous updates.

### 4. Understanding AI Telemetry & AR Overlays
As the AI detects anomalies, it provides immediate feedback in two ways:

* **AR Bounding Boxes Overlay**: Detected red blood cells, white blood cells, or anomalies (e.g., rouleaux, echinocytes) are highlighted directly on top of the live video feed. Each colored box includes a label and a percentage indicating the AI's confidence level.
* **AI Telemetry Panel**: Located on the right side of the screen, this panel acts as a running ledger. Every anomaly and key finding generated by the AI is logged here sequentially. You can review this list to track the overall health indicated by the sample.

### 5. Generating Patient Protocols
Once you have finished analyzing the slide and the AI Telemetry panel is populated with findings:
1. Ensure **Autonomous Analysis** is stopped, so the final list of findings is static.
2. Review the findings in the **AI Telemetry** panel.
3. Click the cyan **"Finalize & Generate Protocol"** button at the bottom of the telemetry panel.
4. **Agent NEXUS** will instantly take those clinical findings and generate a structured presentation and clinical notes (patient protocol), sending them straight to the patient's file.

*Note: Always verify critical AI findings with your own clinical expertise. The AI is designed as a high-powered assistive tool, cross-referencing findings against our proprietary RAG image database for world-class accuracy.*
