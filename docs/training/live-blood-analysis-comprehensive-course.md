## Module 3: Red Blood Cell Analysis (6 Hours)

### 3.1 Healthy RBC Morphology
- Normal size: 7-8 micrometers
- Biconcave disc shape with central pallor
- Smooth membrane appearance
- Free-flowing, non-aggregated
- Uniform staining characteristics

### 3.2 Abnormal RBC Findings

**Rouleaux Formation (Stacking)**
- Appearance: Coins stacked in rolls
- Causes: Inflammation, high protein, dehydration
- Clinical significance: Poor circulation, oxygen delivery
- Nutritional associations: Protein digestion issues
- Differentiate from agglutination

**Echinocytes (Crenated/Spiky Cells)**
- Appearance: Evenly distributed spicules
- Causes: Oxidative stress, fatty acid imbalance
- Associated conditions: Liver stress, uremia
- Differentiate from artifact (old sample)

**Acanthocytes (Spur Cells)**
- Appearance: Irregular, unevenly distributed spicules
- Causes: Severe liver disease, lipid abnormalities
- Clinical significance: Membrane lipid composition

**Target Cells (Codocytes)**
- Appearance: Bulls-eye pattern
- Causes: Liver disease, hemoglobinopathies
- Associated with: Iron deficiency, thalassemia

**Poikilocytosis (Shape Variations)**
- Tear drops, sickle cells, helmet cells
- Each shape has specific clinical associations
- Multiple abnormalities indicate systemic issues

### 3.3 RBC Activity Assessment
- Vitality scoring (1-10 scale)
- Membrane flexibility observations
- Oxygen-carrying capacity indicators
- Lifespan estimation markers
